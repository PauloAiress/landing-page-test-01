Remeber to Drink Water

Staying hydrated is a daily necessity, unfortunately, many of us aren't getting enough to drink. Water keeps every system in the body functioning properly

Top 3 Benefits

1. Helps maximize physical performance. If you don’t stay hydrated, your physical performance can suffer. This is particularly important during intense exercise or high heat. Dehydration can have a noticeable effect if you lose as little as 2% of your body’s water content, this can lead to altered body temperature control, reduced motivation, and increased fatigue.

2. Significantly affects energy levels and brain function. Your brain is strongly influenced by your hydration status. Studies show that even mild dehydration, such as the loss of 1–3% of body weight, can impair many aspects of brain function.

3. May help prevent and treat headaches. Dehydration can trigger headaches and migraine in some individuals.Research has shown that a headache is one of the most common symptoms of dehydration. For example, a study in 393 people found that 40% of the participants experienced a headache as a result of dehydration.

First grid

To function properly, all the cells and organs of the body need water.

It delivers oxygen:

Blood is more than 90 percent water, and blood carries oxygen to different parts of the body.

Boosts skin health:

With dehydration, the skin can become more vulnerable to skin disorders and premature wrinkling.

Controls body heat:

Water that is stored in the middle layers of the skin comes to the skin’s surface as sweat when the body heats up. As it evaporates, it cools the body.

Second grid

The human body comprises around 60% water.

Weight loss:

Water may also help with weight loss, if it is consumed instead of sweetened juices and sodas. “Preloading” with water before meals can help prevent overeating by creating a sense of fullness.

Minimizes hangover:

When partying, unsweetened soda water with ice and lemon alternated with alcoholic drinks can help prevent overconsumption of alcohol.

Helps kidney health:

The kidneys regulate fluid in the body. Insufficient water can lead to kidney stones and other problems.
